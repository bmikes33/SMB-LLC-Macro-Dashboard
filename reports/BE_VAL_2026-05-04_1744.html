Why Young Growth (User Override)

BE explicitly states in the Q1 2026 10-Q that "the CODM reviews consolidated results when making decisions about allocating resources and assessing the performance of the Company as a whole and hence, we have only one reportable segment." Single segment → Consolidated mode (no SOTP scaffolding).

Default lifecycle classification would be High Growth based on margin profile (just turned operating profitable in Q4 2025; Q1 2026 GAAP op income $72M; Adj EBITDA margin ~19%). User override to Young Growth recognizes that:

  • Revenue growth at +130% YoY (Q1 2026) is 3–4× above the standard High Growth band of 20–40%.
  • Margin profile is just-emerging — consolidating Y1 to High Growth treatment via EV/Gross Profit would understate the run-rate revenue scaling story.
  • EV/Revenue is the cleanest metric for AI-tailwind growth narratives where revenue trajectory drives the equity story.

The override is deliberate but worth flagging: using Young Growth multiples on a company with positive margins systematically inflates fair value vs the High Growth EV/Gross Profit framework. Even with this charitable framing, the result is OVERVALUED.
