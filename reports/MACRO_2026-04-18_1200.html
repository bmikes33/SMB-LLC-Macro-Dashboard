3 · Supporting Analysis

3A · Credit Read

Unambiguous risk appetite. Both HYG and LQD V-shaped off their recent lows and reclaimed key pivots — HYG back above 80.50, LQD back above 110. The ratio is rising as HY takes the lead off the bottom, the textbook sign that credit is re-engaging rather than rotating to quality. Both instruments sit above their 200 SMAs with rising 21 EMAs and 50 SMAs underneath. For the credit group to flip, HYG would need to lose 79 convincingly and take LQD below 108 with it — nothing on the tape currently threatens that.

3B · Rates Read

The most interesting group. The front end (US02Y at 3.71%) has pulled back dovishly under 3.75% while the back end (US30Y at 4.89%) remains pinned near 5%. 2s10s is a positive +54 bps with recent bull-steepening character — the canonical Reflation signature. Layered on top, MOVE at 65.7 is a full-on collapse of rates vol (down from the 118 spike), DXY is soft under 100, and USDJPY is stable under 160 with no carry-unwind stress. JP10Y at 2.42% is elevated but not spiking, so no global risk-off amplifier from Tokyo.

The group’s internal split is constructive, not contradictory: MOVE/DXY/USDJPY read clean Risk-On, while 2Y/10Y/2s10s lean Reflation. Both regimes are risk-positive for equities. The real watch is US10Y — a break back above 4.50% would flip the rates group materially and re-assert the Stagflation read from prior weeks.

3C · Volatility Read

Full compression across the vol complex. VIX collapsed from the 33 high to 17.47 — a classic post-stress washout. VVIX punctured the 95 complacency level from above, meaning the vol-of-vol market is no longer positioned for another shock. The SPY IV term structure is clean upward-sloping contango (10% front to 17% at one month), with no kink near the FOMC. This is the quietest the vol complex has been since the last cycle of stress began. The only caveat: compressed vol is also the setup from which vol can expand quickly on a surprise catalyst — FOMC language on Apr 29 is the first real test.

3D · Cross-Asset Tensions

Risk-On with Reflation undertones. Credit and Vol are unanimous Risk-On; Rates is split between Risk-On (MOVE/DXY/USDJPY) and Reflation (2s10s bull-steepening, dovish 2Y). Both are bullish-equity regimes, so the tension is directional agreement with different flavor. If the 10Y continues to drift lower and oil cooperates, Rates migrates fully to Reflation and cyclicals/small-caps should lead. If oil re-engages or CPI surprises hot, the 10Y backs up and Rates flips back toward Stagflation.
