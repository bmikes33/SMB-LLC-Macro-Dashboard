03 Margin & D&A Allocation (the key methodology choice here)

NVIDIA discloses no segment-level profit, so segment EBITDA margins are allocated, not disclosed. Two-step anchor:

  • Edge ≈ 35% EBITDA margin † — structurally lower (gaming/PC/console/workstation/auto/robotics; ~50–55% GM, far higher opex intensity than Data Center).
  • Data Center (Hyperscale = ACIE) ≈ 68% † — same Blackwell architecture across both sub-markets, so no basis to split margin between them. They differ on growth, cyclicality, and assigned multiple — not margin.
  • Internal consistency check: 92.2% × 68% + 7.8% × 35% = 65.4%, reproducing the disclosed 65.6% consolidated operating margin. D&A is immaterial (capex ~1% of EBITDA; asset-light), so EBITDA ≈ EBIT.
Consequence: because HS and ACIE carry the same margin, the entire value difference between them flows from the multiple assigned (Section 4). That makes the ACIE-premium assumption the second-most-important lever after the Data Center base multiple.
