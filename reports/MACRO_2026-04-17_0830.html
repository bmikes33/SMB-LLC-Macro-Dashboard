3 · Supporting Analysis
3A · Credit Read

HYG and LQD both under pressure — differently. HYG is grinding lower with spreads slowly widening, classic late-cycle credit behavior. LQD is being dragged by duration as long-end yields rise. The HYG/LQD ratio is the cleanest tell: ratio is stable but volatile, which isn't a structural breakdown yet — but it is a warning that credit stress is building. If HYG cracks the $75 level, the ratio breaks down meaningfully and credit flips from Stagflation-supportive to Risk-Off-supportive.

3B · Rates Read

Classic bear-steepener signature. Long end (30Y) outpacing the belly (10Y), with the front end (2Y) anchored. That combination — short-rate stickiness + long-end pressure — is the clearest rates signal for Stagflation. The dollar weakening despite higher yields is the key confirmation: in a Risk-Off scenario, rising yields would pull DXY higher. Here, the market is saying "the Fed won't cut enough, and the US growth premium is eroding," which is the exact rates signature John Murphy would classify as Stagflationary.

JP10Y is the under-appreciated signal. At 1.35% and rising, BOJ yield curve control continues to fray. The upside: USDJPY at 152 shows carry is still on, which is why we don't have a Risk-Off escalation yet. The downside: any disorderly move above 155 flips the entire regime to Risk-Off overnight as yen carry unwinds. Monitor daily.

3C · Volatility Read

Equity vol is lagging the rates/credit stress. VIX at 17 and VVIX below 100 are Risk-On readings — but they're increasingly disconnected from what MOVE (112) and credit are saying. This is the primary cross-asset tension in this read. Historically, extended divergences between compressed equity vol and elevated rate/credit vol resolve in one of two ways: (1) rates/credit calm down and equity vol stays low (Risk-On confirms), or (2) equity vol snaps higher to catch up (Risk-Off emerges quickly). The SKEW/SPY IV profile shows protection is being bid selectively, which suggests institutional positioning is leaning toward scenario #2.

3D · Cross-Asset Tensions
  • Vol vs. Rates/Credit: VIX compressed while MOVE elevated and HYG weak. Equity market is priced for continuity; fixed income is priced for stress. Resolution direction is the key question — most often, vol catches up (bearish for equities).
  • USDJPY vs. everything else: Still elevated carry trade is the only thing keeping this from being a full Risk-Off read. A USDJPY break below 150 would cascade.
  • 2s10s neutral: Curve is giving no signal currently — it's neither steepening enough for Reflation nor inverting further for Risk-Off. Watch for a clear directional break.
