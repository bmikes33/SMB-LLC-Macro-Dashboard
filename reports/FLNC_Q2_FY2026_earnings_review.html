▸ The Real Story — What's Actually Happening
01Hyperscaler MSAs Are the Whole Print
Mgmt described them as "chosen from a process involving multiple participants" = competitive RFP win, not a relationship favor. Initial order Q3 FY26 = imminent revenue inflection. This is the catalyst the FLNC long thesis has waited 12+ months for. The headline rev miss became irrelevant the moment this hit the wire.
02Order Intake Doubled — and It's Real Diversification
$2.0B YTD vs. $1.0B prior year. ~50% from first-time customers. This isn't AES/Siemens captive related-party rev (which collapsed from $167M → $40M YoY in Q2 alone). It's external commercial wins. Third-party revenue actually +60% YoY if you decompose the $464.9M total.
03The $80M "Lost" Revenue — Real but Recoverable
Port shipping delays pushed ~$80M from Q2 → Q3. Mgmt confirmed shipments received in April, no further delays. If you "normalize" Q2 rev to $544.9M, the miss is ~11% rather than ~24%. Don't fully discount the miss — Street estimates assumed they'd execute, and they didn't — but it's a timing problem, not a demand problem.
042H FY26 = 70% of Annual Revenue. Execution Risk Lives Here.
FY26 guide is $3.2–3.6B. H1 actuals = $940M. That leaves ~$2.3–2.7B for H2 = 70% backloaded. CFO says "production in line with plan, deliveries tracking as expected." But this is a setup where any further logistics or commissioning slip resets the entire FY narrative. Watch the Q3 print for confirmation, not just the Q2 victory lap.
05Cash Burned Hard — $300M in 6 Months
Total cash: $714.6M (Sept '25) → $412.9M (Mar '26). Inventory ballooned $455M → $764M (+68%) to support 2H deliveries. FCF: −$285M H1. They used $220M of liquidity in Q2 alone. CFO says additional Q3 inventory build then "rebuilding toward $900M by FY end." If 2H slips, the cash position gets uncomfortable fast.
06OBBBA + Domestic Content = Structural Moat
One Big Beautiful Bill Act: Section 48E ITC for storage through 2034. PFE (Prohibited Foreign Entity) restrictions kick in 2026 — effectively prices Chinese competition out of the US base ITC. FLNC has fully compliant US supply chain (TN cells, AZ enclosures, SC inverters). Section 45X manufacturing credit through 2032. This is the regulatory tailwind underneath the AI data center demand pull.
