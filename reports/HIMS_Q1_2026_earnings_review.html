▸ The Real Story — What the Headlines Hide
01US Revenue Actually Declined −8% YoY
US revenue: $529.9M vs. $578.7M. The 4% total growth is entirely an international/Eucalyptus story — RoW jumped from $7.3M to $78.2M (+969%). Strip out international and the core US business is shrinking. This is the single most important number in the release, and they buried it.
02The Compounded GLP-1 Unwind Is Real and Expensive
$33.5M in restructuring charges (inventory writedowns + third-party costs) from the "2026 US WL Announcement" — pivot to branded GLP-1 after the Novo settlement. They're trading higher-margin compounded volume for lower-margin branded distribution. That's the 800 bps of GM compression.
03Quality-of-Revenue Red Flag
Receivables ballooned from $32.1M → $149.6M in one quarter — a $117M increase against only $22M of YoY revenue growth. Accounts payable also jumped $164M. Working capital flag worth probing on the call (channel stuffing? slow-pay branded GLP-1 partners?).
04OpEx Explosion Outside of Marketing
Marketing was actually down slightly ($222M vs. $231M). But: Ops/Support +53%, Tech/Dev +57%, G&A +126%. Even adjusting for $15M legal settlement + $13M acquisition costs, the cost base is inflating fast.
05The Q2 Guide-Up Is the Bull's Hook
$680–700M is well above Street and implies a sharp re-acceleration. Either branded GLP-1 ramps faster than modeled, or Eucalyptus contribution starts hitting (though guidance excludes Eucalyptus — which is odd given the $137.9M acquisition spend already hit in Q1).
